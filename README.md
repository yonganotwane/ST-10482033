1. Market Problem
One major pain point in the car repair market is the lack of fast, transparent, 
and trustworthy service from garages. Many customers experience long waiting 
times, unclear pricing, and poor communication about repairs. Research shows 
that 72% of customers consider quick service the most important factor when 
choosing an auto repair shop, yet the average service wait time after drop-off 
is about 4.2 hours, with some customers waiting more than 8 hours. 
Trust is also a significant issue in the market. Studies indicate that 41% of 
customers report unnecessary repairs as their top concern, and 38% of 
customers switch repair shops because of poor communication from staff. In 
addition, around 45% of customers are dissatisfied due to unexpected costs or 
lack of communication about repairs.
2. Product / Service Overview
The proposed offering is a car repair garage that focuses on fast, transparent, 
and customer-friendly service. It addresses common problems in the market, 
such as long waiting times, unclear pricing, and poor communication, by 
providing customers with clear cost estimates, quicker repair times, and regular 
updates about their vehicle.
What makes this service different from current solutions is its focus on 
transparency and convenience. Customers will know the price and repair time 
in advance, and they will be kept informed throughout the process. This 
approach builds greater trust, saves time, and improves the overall customer 
experience, making it better than many traditional garages. 
3. Target Market
The core customer segments for the car repair garage include private car 
owners, working professionals, and small business vehicle owners. Private car 
owners form a large segment because most households rely on vehicles for 
daily transport. Working professionals often need fast and reliable repairs
because they depend on their cars to commute to work. Small business owners, 
such as delivery drivers or service providers, also need quick and trustworthy 
maintenance to keep their vehicles running and avoid business delays.
These customers typically value speed, affordability, and transparency, and 
they are more likely to choose a garage that provides clear pricing, good 
communication, and efficient service. 
 
4. Business Model
The company will make money through several revenue streams related to car 
repair and maintenance services.
Revenue Streams:
1. Repair and maintenance services – Customers pay for services such as 
engine repairs, oil changes, brake repairs, and diagnostics. 
2. Sale of spare parts – The garage earns profit by selling and installing car 
parts such as batteries, filters, and brake pads. 
3. Service packages or subscriptions – Customers can subscribe to regular 
maintenance plans that include scheduled servicing at a fixed monthly 
or yearly fee. 
4. Diagnostic fees – Charging a small fee to inspect and identify problems 
in vehicles. Pricing Strategy:
The company will use competitive and transparent pricing. Prices will be similar 
to other garages in the area but will include clear upfront quotes, so customers 
know the cost before repairs begin. Discounts or package deals may also be 
offered for regular customers or subscription service plans. 
5. Go-To Market Strategy
The company will reach early customers through a combination of digital 
marketing, local partnerships, and direct promotion.
Initial Channels:
1. Social media and digital ads – Advertising on platforms like Facebook, 
Instagram, and WhatsApp to promote services, special offers, and 
customer reviews. 
2. Local partnerships – Partnering with car dealerships, taxi drivers, and 
small delivery businesses that regularly need vehicle repairs and 
maintenance. 
3. Direct sales and promotions – Distributing flyers, offering opening 
discounts, and promoting services in local communities. User 
Acquisition Tactics:
The garage will attract new customers by offering first-time customer 
discounts, referral rewards, and excellent customer service so satisfied 
customers recommend the garage to others. Positive online reviews and 
wordof-mouth will also help grow the customer base. 
6. Funding Requirements
The business requires approximately R50,000 in funding for the first 12 months to 
start and operate the car repair garage.
Funding Breakdown:
1. Equipment and tools – R20,000
This will be used to buy essential garage equipment such as lifts, 
diagnostic machines, and repair tools. 
2. Operations R15,000
This will cover rent for the garage space, utilities, and salaries for 
mechanics during the early stages of the business. 
3. Marketing – R10,000
Funds will be used for online advertising, social media promotions, flyers, 
and opening promotions to attract early customers. 
4. Spare parts and supplies – R5,000
This will be used to purchase initial car parts, oils, and other materials 
needed for repairs. 
This funding will support the business during its first year of operation, allowing it 
to set up properly, attract customers, and begin generating steady revenue
